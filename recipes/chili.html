Chili

Description

This is as an easy weekday recipe that is cheap to make and can last for 2-3 dinners.

It can be served with cheesy quesedilas, or rice to stretch it even further.

Most the the ingredients can be adjusted to taste, or depending on what one has in their pantry

Ingredients

  • Splash of olive oil
  • 1 clove or garlic
  • 1 red/brown onion - chopped roughly
  • 1 medium carrot - cubed
  • 800g tin crushed tomatoes
  • 1L water/veg stock
  • 3 tins of mixed beans
  • 2 cups frozen corn
  • 2tsp smoked paprika
  • 2tsp cumin powder
  • 2tsp dried oregano
  • 2 bay leaves
  • Salt, pepper, dried chili to taste
  • Fresh lime
  • Yoghurt/Sour cream
  • Corriander for garnish

Steps

  1. Heat oil in a cast iron or soup pot. Or any large pot, really
  2. Add onions and carrots to the pot and simmer them on medium heat for a 5-ish minutes. Stir frequently
  3. Add garlic, herbs and spices, stire and let it simmer for 30 seconds
  4. Pour in water/veg stock and the crushed tomatoes. Stir and than add the beans, corn and bayleaves
  5. Stir it ever so often and bring to simmer
  6. Let it simmer for at least 20-30 minutes. Stir it a few times
  7. Take it off the heat, remove bay leaves. Adjust the seasoning, if needed
  8. If consitency is not thick enough, use a stick-blender and pulse it 2-3 times
  9. Garnish it with a dollop of sour-cream, splash of lime and corriander
